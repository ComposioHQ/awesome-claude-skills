# Social Media Thread Optimizer

This skill converts your blog posts, articles, and long-form ideas into viral-ready social media threads optimized for Twitter, LinkedIn, and other platforms—maximizing reach and engagement without losing depth.

## When to Use This Skill

- Converting blog posts into Twitter/X threads
- Breaking down articles into LinkedIn carousel posts
- Creating thought leadership threads from your research
- Turning internal documentation into shareable threads
- Repurposing webinar transcripts into social content
- Developing thread series from book chapters or guides
- Creating educational threads on your expertise
- Converting case studies into engagement-driving narratives
- Building subscriber anticipation with teaser threads
- Creating thread-based mini-courses or tutorials
- Optimizing existing threads for better performance
- A/B testing different thread angles

## What This Skill Does

1. **Intelligent Content Breaking**: Splits long-form content into perfect-length tweets (280 chars)
2. **Hook Generation**: Creates attention-grabbing opening tweets that drive clicks
3. **Story Structuring**: Arranges content for maximum engagement and information flow
4. **Call-to-Action Optimization**: Places CTAs strategically throughout the thread
5. **Thread Formatting**: Adds proper numbering, spacing, and visual hierarchy
6. **Platform-Specific Adaptation**: Optimizes for Twitter vs. LinkedIn conventions
7. **Engagement Prediction**: Highlights high-impact tweets for maximum reach
8. **Thread Variants**: Generates multiple angle options for A/B testing
9. **Character Optimization**: Ensures tweets hit engagement while respecting limits
10. **Link Placement Strategy**: Determines optimal placement for external links

## Instructions

When a user requests thread creation:

1. **Understand the Source Material & Goal**
   
   Ask clarifying questions:
   - What's the source? (blog post, article, research, original idea, case study)
   - Target platform? (Twitter, LinkedIn, or both)
   - Primary goal? (educate, inspire, establish authority, drive traffic, entertain)
   - Target audience? (developers, marketers, founders, general business)
   - Tone preference? (professional, casual, humor-heavy, storytelling)
   - Any links you want to drive traffic to?
   - Do you want multiple angle options to A/B test?
   - Thread length preference? (short 3-5 tweets vs. comprehensive 15+ tweets)

2. **Analyze Content & Identify Key Insights**
   
   Break down the material:
   
   ```markdown
   # Thread Planning: [Topic]
   
   ## Source Material Analysis
   - Type: [blog post / article / research / case study]
   - Main Theme: [core message]
   - Key Takeaways: [3-5 main points]
   - Potential Hook Angles: [3-5 different ways to open]
   
   ## Target Audience Mapping
   - Primary Audience: [who benefits most]
   - Objections to Address: [what they might doubt]
   - Desired Action: [what you want them to do]
   - Platform Norms: [Twitter = fast-paced, LinkedIn = professional depth]
   ```

3. **Develop Hook Strategy**
   
   Create multiple hook options:
   
   ```markdown
   ## Hook Options (Choose 1 or Test Both)
   
   ### 🎣 HOOK A: Story-Driven
   > "I spent 6 months analyzing [topic]. Here's what I learned:👇"
   - Why it works: Creates curiosity, establishes credentials
   - Best for: Building personal brand authority
   - Engagement potential: High curiosity factor
   
   ### 🎣 HOOK B: Contrarian/Surprising
   > "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong. Here's why: 🧵"
   - Why it works: Challenges assumptions, drives debate
   - Best for: Cutting through noise on crowded topics
   - Warning: Only use if you have solid backing
   
   ### 🎣 HOOK C: Promise-Driven
   > "Read this thread if you want to [specific benefit] 👇"
   - Why it works: Direct value proposition
   - Best for: Educational or how-to content
   - Engagement potential: Attracts interested audience
   
   ### 🎣 HOOK D: Statistics/Bold Claim
   > "[Shocking statistic] and most people don't realize it"
   - Why it works: Creates immediate impact
   - Best for: Data-backed insights
   - Risk: Make sure stat is true and from credible source
   
   ### 🎣 HOOK E: Question
   > "Why do [X] companies do [Y]? I found the answer: 🧵"
   - Why it works: Activates brain, creates engagement
   - Best for: Deeper analysis or counterintuitive findings
   ```

4. **Structure the Thread**
   
   Create the thread outline:
   
   ```markdown
   ## Thread Structure
   
   **Tweet 1** (Hook): [opening tweet to catch attention]
   - Goal: Get readers to open thread
   - Tone: Intriguing, authoritative
   
   **Tweet 2-3** (Setup): [establish context and credibility]
   - Goal: Explain why reader should keep reading
   - Include: Brief background or credentials
   
   **Tweet 4-8** (Main Content): [bulk of insights/value]
   - Break into digestible pieces
   - One idea per tweet
   - Include: Evidence, examples, or data where relevant
   
   **Tweet 9-10** (Deepening): [surprising insight or additional layer]
   - Goal: Provide "aha" moment
   - Make reader think or learn something new
   
   **Tweet 11** (Recap): [tie together main points]
   - Goal: Reinforce key takeaway
   - Include: What to remember
   
   **Tweet 12** (CTA): [next action]
   - Goal: Direct engagement
   - Options: Like/share/comment/follow/click link
   ```

6. **Optimize for Engagement**
   
   Mark high-impact tweets:
   
   ```markdown
   ## Engagement Optimization
   
   ### 📈 HIGHEST ENGAGEMENT TWEETS
   These tweets should get most attention—pin the thread starter:
   
   **Pin Tweet** (Tweet 1):
   I spent 6 months studying [topic]. Here's what changed my understanding...
   - Why: Best hook gets retweets and extends reach
   - Timing: Post at 9-10 AM ET for best visibility
   
   **High Engagement** (Tweet 5):
   The counterintuitive part: [surprising insight]
   - Why: Creates surprise/intrigue
   - Likely reactions: Replies, disagreement, shares
   
   **High Engagement** (Tweet 8):
   If you remember nothing else...
   - Why: Readers seeking value
   - Action: Likely to save or retweet
   
   ### 📊 Character Density Analysis
   - Average chars/tweet: [X] (aim for 150-200 on Twitter for breathing room)
   - Readability score: Clear, scannable formatting
   - Visual breaks: ✓ Using emojis and line breaks strategically
   
   ### 🎯 CTA Placement Strategy
   - Soft CTAs (like, retweet) at Tweet 11
   - Links placed at Tweet 1 and Tweet 12
   - Follow CTA mentioned at Tweet 11
   - Why: Spread CTAs to maintain reader interest
   ```

7. **Create Platform-Specific Variations**
   
   Adapt for different platforms:
   
   ```markdown
   ## Platform Adaptations
   
   ### LinkedIn Version (Carousel Style)
   - Longer tweets (easier on LinkedIn)
   - More professional tone
   - Headshots/credentials early
   - B2B-focused CTAs
   - Focus on career/professional impact
   
   Example LinkedIn version:
   > Slide 1: [Hook + credential]
   > Slide 2-8: [Deeper insights, slightly longer]
   > Slide 9: [Professional CTA - connect, DM, visit website]
   
   ### Twitter/X Optimized Version
   - Shorter, punchier sentences
   - Emoji use for visual breaks
   - Hashtag strategy: 1-3 relevant hashtags
   - Thread counter format: "1/10" 
   - Casual, relatable tone
   
   ### Instagram/TikTok Version
   - Key quotes as graphics
   - Story-driven narrative
   - Shorter attention spans
   - More personality/humor
   - Link in bio for CTAs
   ```

8. **Provide Multiple Angle Options**
   
   Generate A/B test variants:
   
   ```markdown
   ## Thread Variants (A/B Test These)
   
   ### VARIANT A: Authority Angle
   Hook: "I spent 6 months studying..."
   Tone: Expert, credible, data-backed
   Best for: Establishing thought leadership
   
   ### VARIANT B: Story Angle
   Hook: "Here's what happened when I..."
   Tone: Personal, relatable, journey-based
   Best for: Building emotional connection
   
   ### VARIANT C: Contrarian Angle
   Hook: "Everyone says [myth]. Wrong. Here's why..."
   Tone: Challenging, confident, educational
   Best for: Standing out in crowded topics
   
   ### VARIANT D: Utility Angle
   Hook: "4 things you need to know about [topic]..."
   Tone: Practical, actionable, immediate value
   Best for: Maximum saving/sharing
   
   **Test Recommendation**:
   - Post Variant A at 9 AM ET Tuesday
   - Post Variant B at 9 AM ET Thursday
   - Compare engagement by end of week
   - Double down on winner the following week
   ```

9. **Generate Hashtag Strategy**
   
   Optimize discoverability:
   
   ```markdown
   ## Hashtag Strategy
   
   ### Primary Hashtags (1-2)
   #[Popular topic] — Use biggest, most searched
   #[Niche topic] — More specific, less competition
   
   ### Secondary Hashtags (Optional, 1-2)
   #[Industry] — For broader reach
   #[General category] — If very relevant
   
   ### Location in Thread
   - Tweet 1: Include 1 primary hashtag
   - Tweet 6 or 11: Include additional hashtags
   - DON'T hashtag every tweet (looks spammy)
   
   ### Platform Norms
   - Twitter: 1-3 hashtags total, less is more
   - LinkedIn: 3-5 hashtags acceptable
   - TikTok/Instagram: 10-30 hashtags encouraged
   ```

## Pro Tips

1. **Post at Peak Times**: 9-11 AM ET typically gets 40% more engagement
2. **Finish Threads Early**: Post the whole thread at once, not gradually
3. **Pin Your Hook**: Pin the first tweet for extended visibility
4. **Engage In The Comments**: Reply to top comments to boost thread visibility
5. **Use Emoji Strategically**: 1 emoji per tweet improves CTR by ~15%
6. **Create Shareability**: Make one tweet so good people quote tweet it separately
7. **Always Have CTA**: Whether it's follow, like, or link—always ask for next action
8. **Test & Learn**: A/B test hook angles, posting times, thread lengths
9. **Repurpose Replies**: Your best replies in comments become new threads
10. **Track Metrics**: Monitor saves more than likes—saves = value perception

## Best Practices

### Writing
- One idea per tweet, maximum
- Use 150-200 characters (leave breathing room)
- Include specific examples or data
- Show personality, not just information
- Make tweets independently interesting (not just filler)

### Formatting
- Number each tweet (1/10, 2/10, etc.)
- Use line breaks for readability
- Emoji for visual breaks (don't overdo)
- Bold for emphasis

### Engagement
- Reply to every single meaningful comment early (first hour)
- Ask questions that invite discussion
- Quote tweet high-performing tweets separately
- Engage genuinely with other threads
