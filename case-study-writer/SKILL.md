# Case Study Writer

Converts raw customer input (email reply, call notes, Slack message, survey response) into polished case study content in three formats: long-form website page, short LinkedIn post, and a one-paragraph email insert for social proof touches in outreach sequences.

## When to Use This Skill

- When a customer mentions a specific win (time saved, fine avoided, audit passed)
- After a successful demo that included a real customer story
- When preparing Touch 3 social proof for a new email sequence vertical
- When building the fileflo-marketing/case-studies/ archive

## Output: Three Formats

### Format 1 — Website Case Study (400–600 words)

Structure:
```
Headline: "[Specific Outcome] — How [Company Type] in [State] [Achievement]"
Example: "$46,850 in Avoided Fines — How a 120-Driver Fleet in Tennessee Passed Its DOT Audit"

Section 1: The Challenge (100–150 words)
- Company background (industry, size, geography — no name required if they prefer anonymous)
- The specific problem they had before FileFlo
- What they were using before (spreadsheets, shared drives, nothing)
- The moment they knew something had to change

Section 2: The Solution (100–150 words)
- Which FileFlo features they use most
- How long setup took
- What the first week looked like

Section 3: The Results (150–200 words)
- Lead with the most specific, verifiable metric
- Secondary metrics (time saved, violations caught, audits passed)
- Direct quote from the customer (verbatim, or lightly cleaned for grammar)

Section 4: The Numbers (pull-quote format)
- One key stat formatted large: "[X]% reduction in audit prep time"
- Price reminder: "$299/month — [one avoided fine] paid for [X] years of FileFlo"
```

### Format 2 — LinkedIn Post (150–200 words)

Structure:
- **Line 1**: The most surprising/specific outcome stat (this is the hook — make it undeniable)
- **Lines 2–4**: 2-sentence company background + the specific problem they had
- **Lines 5–7**: How FileFlo fixed it — which feature, how fast
- **Line 8**: The before/after in numbers
- **Lines 9–10**: The customer quote (short, 1 sentence)
- **CTA**: "14-day free trial at getfileflo.com — link in bio"

LinkedIn post rules:
- No hashtags in body (move to end if needed, max 3)
- Line breaks after every 1–2 sentences
- Never start with "I" — start with the stat or the hook
- End with a question to drive comments

### Format 3 — Email Insert (50–80 words)

One paragraph, designed to drop into Touch 3 of an email sequence as social proof:
```
"[Industry/size description] in [state] was tracking [specific compliance element] in spreadsheets.
After switching to FileFlo, they [specific result with numbers].
[Short quote if available.]
They're now [current positive state]."
```

## Instructions

1. Ask for: raw customer input (paste the email, quote, or call notes)
2. Identify the single most specific and credible outcome metric — this is the headline
3. Clarify any unverified claims before writing ("Can you confirm the $46,850 figure?")
4. Write all three formats
5. For the LinkedIn post: draft 2 hook variations and let the user pick
6. Note which email sequence Touch 3 this insert is best suited for (construction/transport/healthcare/staffing)
7. Suggest adding to fileflo-marketing/case-studies/ for archive
