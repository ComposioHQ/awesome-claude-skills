# LinkedIn Outreach Playbook

Produces LinkedIn Sales Navigator prospecting workflows and human-written messaging templates that warm prospects 3 days before email Touch 1. Does NOT use automation — LinkedIn prohibits it. All messaging is human-sent.

## Why LinkedIn First

Sending a cold email after a LinkedIn connection is established lifts reply rates 3–4x. When a prospect sees your name in their inbox, they already have context. The connection request is the introduction; the email is the follow-up.

## LinkedIn Sales Navigator Prospecting Workflow

### Step 1 — Account Search
In Sales Navigator Account search, apply:
- **Industry**: match your ICP vertical exactly
- **Headcount**: 15–250 employees (sweet spot for FileFlo)
- **Geography**: target states (TX, FL, CA, AZ, GA for construction)
- **Keywords**: optional — "OSHA", "compliance", "certifications"

Save account list. Review top 50–100 accounts manually for fit.

### Step 2 — Lead Search Within Saved Accounts
In Sales Navigator Lead search, filter by:
- **Title**: exact strings from ICP builder output
- **Seniority**: Manager, Director, VP, Owner, C-Level
- **In saved account list**: yes

Save leads to a list. Export CSV or note names for Apollo email lookup.

### Step 3 — Apollo Email Lookup
For each saved lead:
- Search name + company in Apollo.io people search
- Pull verified email — add to Instantly CSV import
- Note: do not use Apollo enrichment inside Claude (context overflow)

### Step 4 — Pre-Email Warm-Up (3 Days Before Email T1)

**Day -3: Connection Request**
Personalized 300-character note — no pitch, no product mention:

Template A (shared vertical):
> "Hey [Name] — building connections in [industry] compliance in [city]. Saw you're at [Company]. No pitch, just expanding my network in the space."

Template B (content-based):
> "Hey [Name] — your recent post about [topic] resonated. We're working in the same space. Worth connecting."

Template C (mutual connection / peer):
> "Hey [Name] — [mutual connection] suggested I reach out. Both in [industry] compliance — figured worth a connection."

**Day -1: Engage With Their Content**
If they posted in the last 30 days, leave a genuine comment (2–3 sentences, adds perspective, no pitch):
> "[Specific observation about their post]. We've seen similar [pattern/challenge] in [industry]. Have you found [question related to their point]?"

If no recent posts: skip Day -1 and send T1 email on Day 0 as planned.

**Day 0: Email Touch 1 Fires**
Prospect has seen your name twice on LinkedIn. Email lands with context.

### Step 5 — LinkedIn DM (Touch 5, Day 10)
After 2 weeks of email touches with no reply, send a LinkedIn DM as a channel switch:

> "Hey [Name] — I've sent a few emails about [Company]'s compliance tracking. I know inboxes are chaotic. Just wanted to put a face to the name. If the timing's off, no worries — happy to connect either way."

Never pitch in the DM. Ask for a reply, not a meeting.

## InMail (For Tier-1 Dream Accounts Only)

Use InMail only for your top 10–20 target accounts where you have strong conviction on fit. Lead with the most specific insight you have about their company:

> "Hey [Name] — I was looking at [Company]'s recent [project/permit/news] and noticed you're scaling [activity]. OSHA/DOT compliance tracking tends to break at that stage. We've helped [similar company type] automate it for $299/month. Worth a 15-minute conversation?"

## Output

When triggered, produce for the specified vertical and persona:
1. Account Search filter settings (ready to paste into Sales Navigator)
2. Lead Search filter settings
3. 3 connection request templates (A/B/C variants)
4. Day -1 post comment example
5. Touch 5 LinkedIn DM template
6. InMail template for tier-1 accounts
7. Pre-email warm-up calendar (Day -3, Day -1, Day 0 mapped to email sequence)
