# Cold Email Sequence Builder

Writes a complete multi-channel outreach sequence for one ICP vertical. Built for 2026: radical brevity, pattern interrupt subjects, HeyGen video as Touch 3, LinkedIn warm-up before email Touch 1.

## Why 2026 Outreach Is Different

AI-generated emails flood every inbox. Prospects identify and delete them in under 2 seconds. What cuts through:
- **Radical brevity**: 3–5 sentences max. One question. One CTA. Nothing else.
- **Public data hooks**: Reference something verifiable — OSHA inspection records, DOT FMCSA data, state permit filings. This signals you did real research, not GPT.
- **Multi-channel**: LinkedIn warm-up before Touch 1 lifts reply rates 3–4x.
- **Video (Touch 3)**: HeyGen 30-second AI video gets 50% higher engagement than text at this sequence position.
- **Pattern interrupt subjects**: `"quick question"` outperforms `"OSHA fined a company like yours $47K"` — the latter screams sales blast.
- **Plain text only**: HTML templates get filtered to Promotions. Plain text lands in Primary.

## Sequence Structure (8 Touches / 21 Days)

| # | Channel | Day | Length | Angle |
|---|---------|-----|--------|-------|
| L0 | LinkedIn | -3 | 1 line | Connection request — no pitch |
| L1 | LinkedIn | -1 | 1 line | Comment on their recent post |
| T1 | Email | 0 | 3 sentences | Pain hook — public data reference |
| T2 | Email | +2 | 2 sentences | One question only, no pitch |
| T3 | Email + Video | +2 | 1 sentence + video link | HeyGen 30-sec demo |
| T4 | Email | +3 | 3 sentences | ROI math — specific dollar amounts |
| T5 | LinkedIn | +3 | 2 sentences | DM — different channel |
| T6 | Email | +3 | 2 sentences | Peer displacement / FOMO |
| T7 | Email | +4 | 3 sentences | Value-add resource |
| T8 | Email | +3 | 2 sentences | Permission-based breakup |

## Subject Line Rules

Best performers in 2026:
- `"quick question"` — opens at 40%+
- `"{{firstName}}"` — opens at 45%+
- `"noticed something"` — pattern interrupt, non-salesy
- `"re: {{company}}"` — implies prior context (do NOT use fake RE:)

Rules:
- 2–5 words maximum
- Lowercase preferred
- No exclamation marks, urgency words, or claims
- Always provide 2 A/B variants per touch

## Email Format Rules

- Plain text only (no HTML, no logos, no buttons)
- 50–100 words max per touch
- One CTA per email — never two
- Line 1 must reference something specific to the prospect or their industry
- Sign as a real human — include LinkedIn URL in signature
- Never use "I hope this email finds you well" or similar filler

## Personalization Tokens (Instantly format)

- `{{firstName}}` — first name
- `{{company}}` — company name
- `{{city}}` — city or region
- `{{customVar1}}` — web-researched first-line hook (replaces Apollo enrichment — never enrich inside Claude, it overflows context)

## HeyGen Video Script (Touch 3)

Write a 30-second script for the AI avatar:
- Open: "Hey [First Name] — I'm [Sender] from [Company]."
- Action: "I'm going to [demonstrate specific product action] right now — takes about 60 seconds."
- Show: describe what's happening on screen
- Close: "That's what our customers have ready when [their nightmare scenario]. Link below for a free 14-day trial."
Keep the script under 90 words. One CTA. No fluff.

## Instructions

1. Ask: product name, ICP vertical, top 3 pain points, 2–3 key stats/proof points, CTA (trial/demo/call)
2. Research public enforcement data relevant to that vertical (OSHA, DOT, state agencies)
3. Write T1 first — must reference something verifiable. Iterate until the hook is visceral and specific.
4. Write T2 as one question only. No product mention.
5. Write the HeyGen 30-second script for T3, then the email wrapper
6. Write T4–T7 with varied angles — never the same angle in consecutive touches
7. Write T8 as a permission-based breakup — no desperation, just a clean close
8. Output A/B subject line variants for every touch
9. Flag any touch requiring a custom personalization variable beyond the defaults
