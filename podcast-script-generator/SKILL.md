# Podcast Script Generator

This skill transforms articles, research papers, interviews, and ideas into fully-produced podcast episode scripts—complete with host talking points, segment breaks, guest interactions, and production notes—ready for immediate recording.

## When to Use This Skill

- Converting blog posts into podcast episodes
- Creating scripted vs. improvisational sections for flexibility
- Developing interview guides for guest episodes
- Breaking down white papers into podcast content
- Turning case studies into narrative-driven episodes
- Creating solo episodes from research findings
- Generating podcast series outlines from online courses
- Scripting intro/outro segments and transitions
- Planning segment timing for commercial breaks
- Creating guest prep documents
- Developing multi-episode series from books
- Preparing narrative arcs for storytelling podcasts

## What This Skill Does

1. **Content-to-Script Conversion**: Transforms any content into spoken-word format
2. **Natural Dialogue Writing**: Creates conversations that sound human, not scripted
3. **Segment Structuring**: Breaks content into logical 5-15 minute segments
4. **Pacing Optimization**: Calculates reading time and natural pauses
5. **Production Notes**: Includes audio cues, music suggestions, and timing
6. **Guest Prep Materials**: Generates discussion guides for co-hosts/guests
7. **Transition Writing**: Creates smooth topic-to-segment bridges
8. **CTA Integration**: Places calls-to-action naturally throughout
9. **Timing Calculation**: Estimates episode length based on content
10. **Show Format Templates**: Adapts to interview, solo, narrative, or panel formats

## Instructions

When a user requests a podcast script:

1. **Understand Show Format & Audience**
   
   Ask clarifying questions:
   - Show format? (solo, co-hosted, interview, narrative, roundtable)
   - Target episode length? (15, 20, 30, 45, 60 minutes)
   - Target audience? (professionals, students, hobbyists, general audience)
   - Tone? (professional, conversational, educational, entertaining, story-driven)
   - Source material? (article, research, original thoughts, interview, case study)
   - Do you have a co-host or will this be solo?
   - Should it be fully scripted or semi-scripted with key points?
   - Any existing show format/segment structure?
   - Commercials or sponsorships to work around?

2. **Analyze Content & Calculate Timing**
   
   Breakdown the format:
   
   ```markdown
   # Podcast Script Planning: [Episode Title]
   
   ## Show Format Analysis
   - Format: [solo/interview/narrative]
   - Target Length: 30 minutes
   - Estimated Reading Speed: 140-160 words/minute (spoken)
   - Total Wordcount Budget: ~4,200-4,800 words
   
   ## Content Breakdown
   - Intro/Outro: 3 minutes (300-400 words)
   - Segment 1: [topic] - 8 minutes (1,100 words)
   - Segment 2: [topic] - 8 minutes (1,100 words)
   - Segment 3: [topic] - 8 minutes (1,100 words)
   - Closing/CTA: 3 minutes (300-400 words)
   - **Total: 30 minutes**
   
   ## Tone & Style Markers
   - Primary tone: [conversational/professional/casual]
   - Energy level: [upbeat/measured/storytelling]
   - Pacing: [slow & deliberate / fast & punchy / narrative flow]
   - Personality: [data-driven / story-driven / educational / entertainment-focused]
   ```

3. **Create Show Structure Template**
   
   Build the episode framework:
   
   ```markdown
   ## Episode Structure: [Episode Title]
   
   ### SEGMENT BREAKDOWN
   
   **[0:00 - 2:00] COLD OPEN (2 min)**
   Purpose: Hook listeners immediately
   Content: Intriguing fact, question, or story hook
   
   **[2:00 - 3:30] INTRO & HOUSEKEEPING (1:30 min)**
   - Podcast intro (if applicable)
   - Episode title reveal
   - Host introduction
   - Preview of what's coming
   
   **[3:30 - 11:30] SEGMENT 1: [Main Topic A] (8 min)**
   - Key points to cover
   - Any guest involvement
   - Timing milestones
   
   **[11:30 - 13:30] TRANSITION & SEGMENT 2: [Main Topic B] (2 min intro + 8 min content)**
   - Bridge between topics
   - Why this matters to listeners
   - Main content
   
   **[21:30 - 27:30] SEGMENT 3: [Main Topic C] (6 min)**
   - Deepening insights
   - Practical takeaways
   
   **[27:30 - 29:00] CLOSING & CTA (1:30 min)**
   - Summary of key points
   - Call to action
   - Social/website mention
   - Sign-off
   
   **[29:00 - 30:00] OUTRO/CREDITS (1 min)**
   - Podcast outro music
   - Credits/thanks
   ```

4. **Write Natural-Sounding Host Dialogue**
   
   Generate conversational script:
   
   ```markdown
   ## PODCAST SCRIPT: [Episode Title]
   
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   [MUSIC: Upbeat intro music, 15 seconds]
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   
   **[0:00] COLD OPEN**
   
   HOST: "Last week, I spent three hours analyzing why most podcasts don't make it past 
   episode 12. You know what I found? It's not about audio quality. It's not about 
   consistency. It's something way simpler. And in the next 30 minutes, I'm going to 
   break down exactly what separates successful podcasts from the rest. Let's dive in."
   
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   [MUSIC FADE underneath]
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   
   **[2:00] INTRO**
   
   HOST: "Welcome back to [Podcast Name]. I'm your host, [Name]. Today's episode is 
   all about building an audience for your podcast that actually sticks around. We're 
   talking about retention, engagement, and the one thing every successful podcaster 
   gets right in their first three episodes.
   
   This is a conversation I've been wanting to have for months because I see so many 
   creators putting their heart into podcasting, only to watch their audience disappear. 
   So I'm gonna give you the playbook. Here's what we're covering in today's episode..."
   
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   [MUSIC TRANSITION: Subtle, under 3 seconds]
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   
   **[3:30] SEGMENT 1: THE RETENTION PROBLEM**
   
   HOST: "Let's start with the brutal truth. The average podcast loses 50% of its 
   audience between episode one and episode five. Not between episode one and season 
   two. Episode five. Fifty percent gone.
   
   Now, you might be thinking 'Oh, people just discover the show later.' Nope. I studied 
   the data across 500+ podcasts. Most people who start your podcast in episode one 
   make a decision about coming back for episode two within the first four minutes. Four 
   minutes. That's how long you have to prove you're worth their time.
   
   Think about your own listening habits. You start a new podcast. If it's interesting 
   in the first four minutes, you finish the episode and add it to your queue. If it's 
   not, you're gone. Back to the podcast you were already listening to."
   
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   [PRODUCTION NOTE: Natural pause, 2-3 seconds]
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   
   HOST: "So here's the first insight: Your job isn't to be perfect. Your job is to be 
   interesting enough that people finish these first four minutes. Everything else—audio 
   quality, production value, fancy editing—comes second."
   
   [Continues through segments...]
   
   **[27:30] CLOSING**
   
   HOST: "So here's what we've covered today: First, nail the first four minutes. Make 
   it interesting. Make it worth your listener's time. Second, find your unique angle. 
   Don't try to be Joe Rogan or whoever. Be authentically you. And third, finish what 
   you start. Publish consistent episodes. People will stick around if they know when 
   to expect you.
   
   Here's your homework: Listen to the first four minutes of your last episode. If you 
   were hearing it for the first time, would you listen to episode two? Honest question.
   
   If you want more strategies on this, I'm building a course on podcast growth over at 
   [website]. Link is in the show notes. Subscribe to the podcast if you haven't already, 
   and hit me up on Twitter if you have thoughts. I'm @[handle].
   
   Thanks so much for listening. I'll see you in the next episode."
   
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   [MUSIC: Outro music, 15 seconds]
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   ```

5. **Generate Interview Format Variant**
   
   Create discussion guide for guest episodes:
   
   ```markdown
   ## INTERVIEW GUIDE: [Episode Title]
   
   ### PRE-INTERVIEW PREP
   
   **Guest Name**: [Name]
   **Guest Title/Expertise**: [Role]
   **Episode Focus**: [Main topic]
   **Estimated Length**: 45 minutes
   
   **Host Briefing Points**:
   - Guest background: [2-3 sentence summary]
   - Key talking points guest is known for: [list 3-5]
   - Potential controversial opinions: [what might stir debate]
   - Guest's latest work to reference: [books, research, companies]
   
   ### INTERVIEW FLOW & QUESTION LIST
   
   **[Opening - 5 min]**
   Warm-up questions to get guest comfortable:
   - "So [Name], your background is [field]. What drew you to [topic]?"
   - "When did you first encounter [core issue]?"
   - [Easier questions to build rapport]
   
   **[Main Content - 20 min]**
   Core topic exploration:
   - "You wrote/said [quote]. What did you mean by that?"
   - "Walk us through how you approach [methodology]."
   - "What's the biggest misconception people have about [topic]?"
   - [Deep-dive questions]
   - "What surprised you most while researching this?"
   
   **[Controversial Takes - 10 min]**
   Interesting disagreement areas:
   - "I've heard some people argue [opposite view]. How do you respond?"
   - "What do you think industry experts get wrong about [topic]?"
   - [Push slightly on assumptions—respectfully]
   
   **[Practical Application - 8 min]**
   Listener value:
   - "If someone's listening to this and thinking 'This doesn't apply to me,' 
      what would you say?"
   - "Give us one practical step someone can take this week based on what you've shared."
   - [Actionable advice]
   
   **[Personal/Storytelling - 7 min]**
   Make it human:
   - "Tell us about a time when [theory] failed you in real life."
   - "What's something nobody knows about your path to [success]?"
   - [Personal stories and failure moments]
   
   **[Closing - 5 min]**
   Wrap-up and CTAs:
   - "What should people read/listen to/follow to learn more?"
   - "Where can our audience find you?"
   - [Guest's links and next steps]
   
   ### TALKING POINTS FOR HOST
   - Guest's biggest insight: [summarize main message]
   - Why this matters to listeners: [relevance connection]
   - Most quotable moment: [best soundbite]
   - Call to action: [what to do with this info]
   
   ### INTERVIEW TIPS
   - Give guest space to talk (shoot for 60/40 guest/host)
   - Listen for interesting rabbit holes to explore
   - Keep questions open-ended ("What..." not "Is...")
   - No talking over each other (edit later if needed)
   - Record 10 min intro/outro separately for flexibility
   ```

6. **Create Production Notes**
   
   Add technical guidance:
   
   ```markdown
   ## PRODUCTION NOTES
   
   ### MUSIC & AUDIO
   - Intro Music: [Specify style/energy level, length 15-30 sec]
   - Transition Music: [Subtle, energetic, melancholic, length 3 sec]
   - Outro Music: [Similar to intro, length 15-30 sec]
   - Music sources: Epidemic Sound, Artlist, AudioJungle
   
   ### TIMING MARKERS
   - [0:00] Cold open starts
   - [2:00] Intro begins
   - [3:30] Segment 1 begins
   - [11:30] Segment transition
   - [27:30] Closing begins
   - [29:00] Outro starts
   
   ### COMMERCIAL INTEGRATION
   - [Midroll 1] After segment 1, ~12 min mark [ad read space: 30-60 sec]
   - [Midroll 2] After segment 2, ~18 min mark [ad read space: 30-60 sec]
   
   ### SOUND QUALITY CHECKLIST
   - [ ] Host audio: Clean, consistent levels
   - [ ] Guest audio: Clear, background noise minimal
   - [ ] Music: Properly EQ'd under dialogue
   - [ ] Ambient sound: Removed or intentionally kept
   - [ ] Transitions: Smooth, no dead air over 2 sec
   - [ ] Final mix: All elements at appropriate volume
   
   ### RECORDING SETUP
   - Host microphone: [Specify if USB/XLR, recommended model]
   - Guest connection: [Zoom/Riverside/Streamyard/local recording]
   - Backup recording: [App name/method]
   - File format: [WAV/MP3], [Sample rate: 48kHz or 44.1kHz]
   
   ### POST-PRODUCTION
   - Duration estimate: ~30 minutes
   - Editing time estimate: 2-3 hours
   - Editing tasks:
     - [ ] Remove long pauses (over 3 sec of silence)
     - [ ] Clean mouth clicks and breaths
     - [ ] Normalize audio levels
     - [ ] Add music/transitions
     - [ ] Add intro/outro
     - [ ] Fade in/out music appropriately
     - [ ] QA: Listen through completely
   
   ### EQUIPMENT RECOMMENDATIONS
   - Budget Option: [USB mic + free editing software]
   - Intermediate: [Decent USB mic + Audacity/Adobe]
   - Professional: [XLR setup + Adobe Audition/Logic]
   ```

## Pro Tips

1. **The First 4 Minutes**: Make them count. Hook listeners immediately
2. **Natural Pauses**: 2-3 seconds of silence feels like breathing room; adds 5-10% to listen time
3. **Guest Prep**: Send interview guide 48 hours before—guests perform better with time to think
4. **Scripted Sections**: Intro/outro fully scripted; main content semi-scripted (key points, not full dialogue)
5. **Editing for Tightness**: Remove pauses over 3 seconds; keep natural "ums" for authenticity (not all)
6. **Recording Time**: Plan 1-2 hours to record 30 minutes of final content (includes retakes)
7. **Consistent Upload**: Same day/time each week builds listening habits
8. **Show Notes Matter**: Comprehensive show notes improve SEO and offer extra value
9. **Mirror Your Blog**: Podcasts should expand on blog content, not replace it
10. **Audio Quality**: Decent USB mic (~$100) is better than free software on pro XLR setup

## Best Practices

### Writing for Spoken Word
- Use short sentences (under 20 words)
- Write for the ear, not the eye
- Include natural transitions ("So here's the thing...")
- Use contractions ("You're" not "You are")
- Vary sentence length for natural rhythm
- Read aloud before recording—if you stumble, rewrite

### Managing Guests
- Send interview guide 48 hours before
- Ask guests their pronunciation preference/bio preferred wording
- Record intro/outro separately for flexibility
- Save guest audio separately from host
- Respect guest's time (don't run long)

### Post-Production
- Keep ambient noise minimal (quiet room)
- Normalize audio levels between all speakers
- Remove dead air (silence over 2-3 seconds)
- Clean up mouth clicks (but keep natural voice character)
- Use music to signal transitions
- QA by listening completely through
